Investment thesis
SA is a structurally bifurcated market — dispatch flexibility and timing earn premium revenue while raw generation volume does not. The optimal portfolio is a 60/40 wind-plus-storage hybrid, supplemented by selective solar only where firm contracts or co-located storage protect revenue.

Solar cannibalisation

Critical framing
The challenge facing SA renewables is not operational underperformance — assets hit resource targets. The problem is purely revenue: fix via contracting and storage co-location, not technology switching.
Battery net revenue modelled via rule-based arbitrage: charge bottom-12 daily intervals, discharge top-12, RTE = 0.85. Monthly battery revenue oscillates $8k–$42k/MW vs wind $5k–$25k and solar $0–$15k.
Risk register
Storage saturation — faster-than-modelled battery CF compression as SA storage queue commissions 2025–2027
Policy reversal — Federal CIS or state scheme changes reducing contracted revenue floors for new renewables
Connection delays — transmission build and connection queue delays affecting deployment timelines and IRR
Gas price volatility — domestic gas movements affecting peaker economics and low-VRE price formation
Resource scarcity events — multi-month wind/solar droughts may be under-represented in the 2-year window
